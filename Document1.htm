Chapter One: The Man in the Leather Coat

“Hello there” came a husky, bass voice. I looked up for the first time in the last twenty minutes and blinked uneasily trying to adjust my sight to the scorching afternoon sunlight beaming in its raging splendour and the blurry figure in front of me. Maybe I should have gone inside the café but I just needed some space and privacy which the indoors of Mc Allen café wouldn’t provide so I had to manage the outdoor table.

 

“Can I sit here please?” A man, not more than 45 years old and dressed in a leather coat asked, poking his head comically in the large umbrella attached to the table I sat at.

 

“Um…sure, Good day sir” I replied, respecting the obvious age difference between us.

 

“Just call me Edwin, Edwin Bello” he said with a nod, after which he dropped his surprisingly heaped lunch tray and scurried over to another table close by to get a chair which he slumped heavily into after dragging it across the ground over to my table, making me jolt. As if in apology, he let out a wide smile but there was something about his presence and toothy grin that did not sit right with me. I adjusted uncomfortably in my chair and feigned a tight-lipped smile before ducking my gaze back into the novel I had been reading.

 

Another 10 minutes or so passed, the air filled with Mr. Edwin noisily smacking on a pile of donuts. I exhaled, peace and quiet were two of the major reasons I chose the outdoor table.

 

“My friend was right when he warned me about these” He said, referring to the donuts “Know what he said?” Mr. Edwin asked, I shook my head, completely uninterested “He said if you’ve never had ‘em before, don’t! unless you want to change your life” he said and laughed with a loud chortle.

 

I smile untruthfully to ease the tension “Good old Ben, was quite a jolly man” he said “until his wife went missing that is, she was one of those ladies that just lo-o-oved looking trendy” He added and if I didn’t know better, I’d say he sounded rather disapproving.

 

“Sorry to hear” I replied, focusing my gaze at the pages of my book.

 

He exhaled, bit into another donut and continued “Potiskum, really doesn’t change much and I’d know, I’ve been here since I was a toothless baby and I’ll probably stay till the teeth go away again” he said and chuckled

 

“If you continue stuffing yourself with bagels and other junk, that day’ll come faster than you think” I thought with a smirk as I sipped from my coffee mug. I checked my wristwatch. It was 12:00 PM. “twenty more minutes till my break’s over” I thought.

 

“What?” I asked, looking up, unsure of what he had just said or if at all he had said anything.

 

“I asked if you were a student” he repeated.

 

“An intern actually” I replied and sipped more coffee.

 

“I didn’t catch your name earlier”

 

“You didn’t actually ask but it’s Valeria, Valeria Onaraku sir” I replied, scratching my left eyebrow. I had left the office to escape conversation with people and here the universe was ruining my plan, thanks a lot mother nature!

 

Don’t get me wrong. My co-workers are all nice and friendly-but that’s the problem. I’m kind of a loner and the whole jolly-clingy mood doesn’t appeal to me. Therefore, I don’t fit into the class of the other cheerful techies I usually spend the office hours tapping away at my keyboard, working on some hit project or the other, never taking a rest or leaving my cubicle unless for a pee break and leaving the office immediately after the closing alarm, ignoring any small talk in the process. It wasn’t always like that, I think it started precisely after my best friend, Julia disappeared. We had gone hiking that day (Yep, we liked to do a few things boys usually enjoyed) and she had begged to let her wear the new outfit I bought. I had no problem with that because to tell you the truth, it had come as a mix-up with a bunch of clothes I ordered so I let her have it. I didn’t like clothes that were in vogue, they’d make you blend in like a millionth twin!

 

The mountain was located in Azare, Yobe state. Youth corps members usually went training there during their three weeks of camping. We saw a few of them on our way to the top along with a few other hikers.

 

“Corper wee!” we hailed at some fainting ones to motivate them. When I had done my camp hike, it didn’t hurt because I was already used to it.

 

Other hikers kept coming and going but there was one particular man I couldn’t help but notice each time I turned around, he was in a camo bumper jacket with a smudge of what looked like jelly, cargo pants, a face mask and dark shades. He wasn’t doing anything peculiar just occasionally looking through his binoculars but the weird thing was he was always just there, taking the same route as us but walking suspiciously slow.

 

“Maybe some homeless guy” I thought. When we reached the top, Julia decided to get some sticks for a fire, insisting we spent the night outdoors. I offered to go with but she declined, suggesting instead that I set up camp while she was away, after which she left. I did as I was asked and put up our tent and other things. After a while, Julia still hadn’t returned.

 

“She should have been back ages ago” I thought and decided to go look for her. I had walked a short distance when I noticed some twigs scattered on the ground, they looked like they had been gathered but dropped. Being a fan of crime tv, an instinct kicked in; something was definitely wrong “Julia! Julia?! Jul…” I called out while looking around frantically, the last part trailed off when I noticed, laying at the foot of a tree, an orange jacket. Panic took over, I rushed to it and picked it up. There was an unmistakable and self-stitched star embroidery on its left arm, I had done that it! It was Julia’s! I dropped it immediately and looked around in horror, then my eyes caught another sight that sent me half-mad. On the same tree was tied a piece of the blue shirt she wore now tipped with blood stains on parts of it. My vision blurred out at that, it was all it took for me to plunge into the cold waters of unconsciousness.

 

Waking up and adjusting to my surrounding, I stirred in bed and noticed the four pairs of eyes staring down at me; that of my parents-and Julia’s.

 

“Val, you’re finally awake” My mother started while rubbing my short hair.“Some hikers brought you back home yesterday, they said they found you unconscious, where did you leave Julia?” I swallowed hard, sitting up.

 

“She asked you a question?!” Julia’s mum roared amidst sobs. I told them all I knew and in no time, they contacted the police and mobilized search parties to find her but I knew deep within that they wouldn’t. It’s been two years since then and though, no one ever says anything, the look on their faces when they get informed says it all. I know they blame me, they all do.

 

I shook my head coming out of my cloud of thoughts. Mr. Edwin must have been talking for ages, what did I miss?

 

“Sir?” I asked and he laughed.

 

“You seem to drift off pretty fast, I asked if you were for the arts in your internship” he replied.

 

“Um…no, I’m for ICT”

 

“Then why’re you studying throwback novels so intently?” he asked biting into yet another donut.

 

“It’s just for the sake of it, I find novels captivating” I replied truthfully.

 

“You know, I read that novel when it was first published” he started.

 

“Did you?” I asked rhetorically, trying to sound as deterring as I could but he did not take the hint.

 

“Yes, some time in the 1990s when I was a youngster” he explained. “There’s something quite special about that book, Danger at the cliff”.

 

Of course, the book was special, its now deceased author was one of the best in Yobe state and Nigeria as a whole, not to mention it was from the 19s and it had a 4-star rating but I had a gut feeling that these were not what he meant.

 

“It really spoke to me” he said as his eyes lit up. “Especially the characters Margaret and Sean” I sat up at this, becoming genuinely interested.

 

The character Margaret was murdered by the untraditional protagonist Sean, a backwards, depraved psycho killer for no other reason than her choice to wear clothing he emphasized were traditionally for men.

 

“You know the funny thing is that since the time that novel was published and its first play was acted till now, you’d expect the womenfolk to cower and dress more…womanly” he continued, I adjusted uneasily, remembering I was in trousers.

 

“Religious critic” I thought.

 

“But instead it basically encouraged them so much so that the exact clothes the actor who played Margaret wore in the scene where she died, became a wide spread fashion article that was a must have for almost every lady” he said with an irritated look and continued “you’d think it’d die down after a while but it didn’t, even after 30 years or so, the lunacy carried on” he said and bit a large chunk.

 

I’d begun to feel irritated by his presence.

 

“I lived here when the whole trend started, grew with it till my tolerance reached its limit”.

 

I looked at him as he bit into another donut.

 

“So, I decided to take…control” his eyes narrowed as he said that “What do you mean by that?” I asked, narrowing my eyes quizzically at him.

 

“I saw myself as Sean, I had the responsibility to rid this town of the new insolent Margarets. Of course, almost every lady in town was guilty but I decided to go for those who wanted to look like Margaret, those ladies that wore the outfit from the first play” he paused “because if they wanted to be like Margaret, I was going to help them” he explained with what one could call passion in his wide eyes.

 

“For my first Margaret, I did it exactly by the book…how Sean did it. I stalked her for a few weeks, waiting for the right moment to strike”

 

12:10 PM

 

“And that moment came on a day she had visited her lover. It didn’t take much to do, just a quick snuff out and two or more silent hits across her head with a shovel. She didn’t even get the chance to scream before it happened and let me tell you something” he said and leaned closer.

 

“There was nothing as exhilarating as looking at that shattered, once life-filled head with all the blood and gore spilling from it, her wide frightened eyes were rolled back and her skin was turning grey” he added, shut his eyes, squealed like it was the most normal thing to say and fell back into his chair.

 

I struggled with the rising bile in my throat.

 

“Of course, being learned, I’d made sure not to leave any prints but you know the saying ‘cut off one head and two more will take its place’ from the herculean tasks?”

 

I nodded.

 

“Well, that’s what kept happening so I got more creative and made my strategy more exciting and…breathtaking” he said and snapped his fingers as he remembered and said the last word.

 

“For each victim, I always did the same thing; snuffed her out and smuggled her to my farmhouse, made sure she had her final-though forceful-sexual experience. Even if she didn’t want it, there was no way I’d leave a lady unsatisfied” he said and chuckled as I turned pale.

 

“It didn’t matter how old she was…26, 12, 30, 9, 60 maybe. No age matters…so I guess I was some ladies’ first” he said and laughed loudly like someone had just made a sitting room joke.

 

“As you can imagine, they’d probably call me a twisted sicko cos of that but I didn’t care-and I still don’t. Next, I’d take a knife and rip the clothes off while detailing to her why I’d captured her which was because of the damn outfit, the whole perky blue T-shirt that said ‘men at work’ and the fucking baggy denims with the fly facing the wrong way for a lady. Then slowly, very, very gently, I’d peel off her frightened face from her skull. The sound from the flesh snapping and disconnecting from the body is just pure magical” he said and closed his eyes like he was dreaming about a Christmas present.

 

“Thereafter, I’d take my trusty axe and chop off her head. Of course, women are blessings and I’m not just going to waste any part of them-except maybe some blood” he joked and I giggled insincerely.

 

“The next thing was her body, I’d cut open her stomach with a scalpel and pluck out all the organs I could get; Intestines, kidney, bronchial cavity, everything and anything I could lay hands on and give my pigs a big, tasty, feminine dinner. Then I’d process the remaining skin into a nice, cosy, leather coat” he said and breathed out, rather smugly.

 

“Since pigs don’t eat bones, I’d give them to Spud, my trusty sheepdog to do justice with. He usually buries the bones on the farm…so I’ve got a really rich soil” he laughed again.

 

“I’ve been about this holy work for roughly 25 years, and it doesn’t seem like it’ll end soon…but I’m committed and I’ll finish the jo…” the opening of the café door interrupted him.

 

A lady in the same cosplay outfit exited and headed for a taxi.

 

“Well, that’s my cue” he said and swallowed the last chunk of his donut before standing up and pulling a mask out of his pocket. He leaned closer to my horror and held my chin, tilting it upwards.

 

“Just don’t be the next Margaret, I like you but if you still want to, I wouldn’t mind helping”

 

He said sinisterly, making me gulp and pull away, then he smirked before leaving the umbrella.

 

My head spun trying to process all I just heard. Then it clicked, my stomach churned and in a split second, my digestive track rewound. Julia was Margaret! 12:20 AM.
